Biomedical Engineering

My work at the intersection of signal processing and physiological monitoring

Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG) Internship

I have contributed to helping build an app focused on mapping remote photoplethysmography recordings to the body's inflammatory response. This work explores how non-contact optical sensors can detect subtle changes in blood flow patterns that correlate with inflammatory markers in the body.

Key Areas of Focus

  • Signal processing techniques for rPPG data extraction and analysis
  • Correlation analysis between optical measurements and inflammatory biomarkers
  • Development of algorithms for real-time physiological monitoring

Applications

This research has potential applications in non-invasive health monitoring, early detection of inflammatory conditions, and remote patient care systems. The ability to assess inflammatory response without direct contact opens new possibilities for continuous health monitoring in various settings.
